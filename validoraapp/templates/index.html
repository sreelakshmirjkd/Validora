What exactly is a "second opinion" for visa agencies and why do I need one?

A second opinion means getting independent verification from visa experts with no financial interest in your decision. If a visa agency is charging you money for jobs, education, or immigration services, they profit from saying "yes." We only profit from telling you the truth about visa processes and agencies. Just like getting a second medical opinion before surgery, get a second opinion before paying visa agencies large sums.

How can I tell if a visa job offer from Europe is fake?

Red flags include: 1) Visa agencies charging ₹50,000+ for "guaranteed" job visas, 2) Job offers for unskilled work with high salaries, 3) Companies willing to sponsor work visas without proper interviews, 4) Requests for upfront visa processing payments. EU law requires most work visas to go through official labor market testing - companies can't just hire anyone from outside EU easily.

Why do education visa consultants sometimes give bad advice?

Many visa consultants earn higher commissions from lower-ranked universities that are desperate for international students. A top university might pay 5-10% commission, while a struggling university might pay 20-30% for student visas. Some consultants prioritize their profit over your visa success and future. We verify university rankings, visa success rates, and consultant motivations.

How do visa-related housing scams work and how can I avoid them?

Common visa housing scams: 1) Fake listings targeting visa applicants with stolen photos, 2) "Landlords" asking for deposits before you get your visa approved, 3) Student accommodation scams for visa students, 4) Properties that don't exist but are marketed to visa holders. Always video call to see the actual property, meet the real landlord, and verify ownership before sending money for visa-related housing.
